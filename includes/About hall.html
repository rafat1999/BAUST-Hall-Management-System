Residential Facilities (Male/Female)

October 13, 2021 views
  • LinkedIn
  • Facebook
  • Twitter

The university believes that campus life is an important aspect in the development process of students. In addition, to provide services in assisting students in solving problems that affect their studies, the university aims to create an environment conductive to cultural development and promotion of interaction among staffs, students and intellectuals. The university has three residential halls for the accommodations of the students.

  • Abbas Uddin Ahmed Hall (AUAH): The hall for the male students is named after the famous Bhawaiya folk song composer and singer of Rangpur Abbas Uddin Ahmed. This hall has 515 seat capacities.
  • Bir Protik Taramon Bibi Hall (BPTBH): The hall for the female students is named after Bir Protik Taramon Bibi who is one of the two female freedom fighters in Bangladesh obtaining the Bir Protik award. This hall has 470 seat capacities.
  • New Hall: This is the hall for the male students. This hall has 300 seat capacities.

The students live in these on community basis, while 3/4 students share a single room. Each hall has a TV room, wide balcony, indoor games and dining room facility. A provost and some assistant provosts administrate each hall.

All halls are neat and clean and possess a perfect environment for study. Top quality security is ensured by our own security guards.

Food quality and nutrition values are also maintained here strictly to preserve the good health of our students.
